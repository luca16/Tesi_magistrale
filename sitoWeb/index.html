The impeachment of Donald Trump

The impeachment of Donald Trump, the 45th president of the United States, occurred on December 18, 2019, when the Democrat-controlled House of Representatives approved articles of impeachment on charges of abuse of power and obstruction of Congress.
Trump was acquitted by the Republican-controlled Senate on these two counts of impeachment on February 5, 2020.



The site relates to a project carried out in the RomaTre University.

The material used for the project was taken exclusively from free sources, in particular the following sources from YouTube have been used.

  • Impeachment Inquiry | NBC News
  • Impeachment Trial Highlights & Analysis | NBC News

The textual material pulled out of the playlists has been used through the semantic analysis software Cogito Intelligence API


Graphs of the various pages are organised as follows :

  • The horizontal axis describes the time, namely the various dates when the videos have been uploaded to YouTube
  • The vertical axis describes the typology of information pulled out


These are little captions that have to be put in each page:

  • Key Events – the page shows a complete timeline of key events regarding the story of Trump’s impeachment.
  • Actors – the page shows the actors who took part in the story of Trump’s impeachment.
  • Actors Correlation – the page shows the most correlated ten actors according to the statistic correlation coefficient.
  • Places – the page shows places and locations quoted in the story of Trump’s impeachment.
  • Relations – the page shows the entities and the respective relations identified in the story of Trump’s impeachment.
  • Categorization – the page shows graphs related to the four domains of interest.
  • Emotions – the page shows emotions and states of mind identified in the story of Trump’s impeachment.
